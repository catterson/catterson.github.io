Lab 4. Self-Esteem Problems

Hi Class! Thanks for your patience with this process today. Apologies it was a bit messier than I had planned; I hope this lab assigment feels useful and not too hard.

Here’s a key for the first two questions in the Lab assignment to guide your work and learning.

I also recorded a video key walking over the interpretation of the interaction effect (summary table and graph). I hope you find these useful in supporting your learning!

Access the self-esteem dataset. Look over the codebook to learn about the variables and how they were measured.

  1. Create a self-esteem scale from the 10-items. Make sure to reverse-score the negatively-keyed items so that for every question, higher numbers measure higher self-esteem.

    • a) Graph this variable as a histogram, and make the graph look nice (ready for publication).

    • b) Report the alpha reliability, mean, and standard deviation of this variable. Below your graph and these statistics, describe what these statistics tell you about the self-esteem of the participants.

    • d) Finally, evaluate the items in the likert scale - does this seem like a valid and reliable way to measure self-esteem? Do you think these questions would generalize to people across the world (why / why not?) What other questions might you have about this variable?

  2. Define a series of linear models to determine :

    • a) whether there is a relationship between self-esteem and age

    • b) whether men and women differ in terms of their self-esteem

    • c) which variable (age or gender) is a better predictor of self-esteem.

    • d) whether the relationship between age and self-esteem depends on the gender of the participant (an interaction effect!).

    • Report the relationship (slopes) and $R^2$ values from your model in a summary table and graph your interaction effect (you do not need to graph the other models).

  3. Using the key as a guide, try to define a different series of linear models to predict SELF-ESTEEM (or the CONSPIRACY BELIEF) scale that professor demonstrated in class from some other variables. Post your work to the VISION BOARD.
