For Next Week.

Lab 5. Political Problems.

Link to Dataset and Research Article. These data come from a published study on how dehumanizing language influences political beliefs. The author wanted to test “how the dehumanization of immigrants influences immigration policy attitudes”. To do this, they conducted an experiment, in which participants read either a dehumanizing or non-dehumanizing political speech about immigrants (variable = dh_treat) and then measured participants attitudes about three separate immigration questions (variables = imm1, imm2, imm3).

  1. Without looking at the results of the original paper, think about what linear model(s) you would define (based on the variables measured) to test the author’s research question (above). Make sure you consider relevant control and / or confound variables to include, and explain why these are needed. Then, define and interpret this linear model. Was dehumanizing political speech related to immigration policy attitudes? Report your results with a graph and summary regression table.

  2. After you complete question 1, look at Table 1 from the original paper. How did the researcher’s linear models differ from your own? See if you can recreate the author’s results using the variables in the dataset (feel free to just focus on one model).
